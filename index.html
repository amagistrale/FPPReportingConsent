Invitation

You are invited to take part in a research project because you have a valid permit at Fitzroy Provincial Park for camping or day-use. The information in this form is intended to help you understand what we are asking of you so that you can decide whether you agree to participate in this study. Your participation in this study is voluntary, and a decision not to participate will not be used against you in any way. As you read this form, and decide whether to participate, please send the lead researcher (Anna) all the questions you might have, take whatever time you need, and consult with others as you wish.


What is the purpose of this study?

This study will benefit Park Managers and maintenance staff by allowing the public to report minor maintenance issues that they see throughout a park. It will determine if using a PPGIS is a feasible way to keep track of maintenance issues in the park and further examine the helpfulness of citizen science in Park Management.


What will I be asked to do?

If you agree to take part in the study, we will ask you to:

  • Fill out a short form detailing when, where, what kind of maintenance issue you observed in the park.
  • Provide your phone number and exact location (location is not mandatory).
  • If necessary, give more detail to Fitzroy Provincial Park staff about what you noticed.

Possible Benefits

You may not receive any direct benefit from your participation in this study. However, your participation may allow researchers to better understand if using PPGIS is a feasible way to keep track of maintenance issues within a park.

Withdrawing from the study

If you withdraw your consent during the course of the study, all information collected from you before your withdrawal will still be used, unless you request that it be removed from the study data. After the study, you may request that your data be removed from the study and deleted by notice given to the Principal Investigator (named above) before December 6, 2024.


Confidentiality

We will remove all identifying information from the study data as soon as possible, which will be after the report for the study has been submitted (December 6, 2024). We will treat your personal information as confidential, although absolute privacy cannot be guaranteed. No information that discloses your identity will be released or published without your specific consent. Data will be shared and used by Fitzroy Provincial Park staff to fix reported maintenance issue and to possibly contact you for more information on the issue. Research records may be accessed by the Carleton University Research Ethics Board in order to ensure continuing ethics compliance. All data will be kept confidential, unless release is required by law (e.g., child abuse, harm to self or others). The results of this study may be published or presented at an academic conference or meeting, but the data will be presented so that it will not be possible to identify any participants unless you give your express consent. We will remove all identifying information from the study data as soon as possible, which will be after December 6th, 2024. Your data will be stored and protected within a Google Spreadsheet and later an ArcGIS Online Layer, but may be disclosed via a court order or data breach.


Data Retention

Your data will be retained until December 6th, 2024, and then securely destroyed. Phone Numbers will be removed from the data upon completion of the issue.
